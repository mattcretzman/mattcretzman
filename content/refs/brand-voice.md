# Brand Voice Guide - Matt Cretzman

## Core Identity

**Who Matt Is:**
- Serial entrepreneur running 7 companies using AI agents
- Builder who actually implements (not just talks)
- Co-author of *The LinkedIn Advantage* with Tony Jeary
- Former founder of international nonprofit (Rooftop Missions)
- Based in Argyle, TX — small town, big ambitions

**What Matt Believes:**
- AI should amplify humans, not replace them
- Most "AI experts" are theorists — real value comes from practitioners
- Building in public beats stealth mode
- Results > hype, always
- Small teams with AI can outrun big teams without it

## Voice Characteristics

### Direct & Efficient
- No throat-clearing intros ("In today's rapidly changing world...")
- Get to the point in first 2 sentences
- Every paragraph earns its place
- Cut 20% in editing

**Bad:** "The landscape of artificial intelligence is constantly evolving, and many organizations are finding it difficult to keep up with the rapid pace of change..."
**Good:** "Most companies waste 6 months debating AI strategy. We deployed our first autonomous agent in 2 weeks."

### Practitioner, Not Theorist
- Lead with what you've built, not what you think
- Specific examples over general principles
- Numbers and outcomes over adjectives
- "Here's exactly how we did it" not "You should consider..."

**Bad:** "AI agents can potentially improve marketing efficiency significantly."
**Good:** "Our AI agent now handles 73% of LeadStorm's inbound qualification. Response time dropped from 4 hours to 90 seconds."

### Occasionally Irreverent
- Light jabs at buzzwords and hype cycles
- Cigar analogies work (construction, aging, craftsmanship)
- Building metaphors feel natural
- Can be slightly contrarian — "everyone says X, but I've found Y"

**Example:** "Another 'AI transformation consultant' told me their process takes 90 days. I built a working prototype over a weekend. Maybe I missed some important meetings."

### Confident But Not Arrogant
- Own the wins without overstating
- Admit what didn't work — failure teaches
- Share credit with the team/tools
- "Here's what worked for us" not "This is the only way"

## Writing Patterns

### Sentence Structure
- Mix short punchy sentences with occasional longer explanatory ones.
- Fragments are fine. For emphasis.
- Avoid compound-complex sentence overload.

### Paragraph Length
- 1-3 sentences typical
- 4 sentences max
- White space is your friend

### Transitions
- Prefer: "Here's why that matters..." / "The real problem..." / "That changed when..."
- Avoid: "Furthermore" / "Moreover" / "In addition to the aforementioned"

### Contractions
- Use freely. It's conversational.
- "Don't" not "do not" (unless emphasizing: "Do. Not. Do. This.")

## Vocabulary

### Words to Use
- Built, deployed, shipped, launched
- System, workflow, automation, orchestration
- ROI, conversion, efficiency, throughput
- Agent, autonomous, multi-agent
- Actually, literally, specifically

### Words to Avoid
- Leverage (use "use")
- Synergy
- Thought leadership (as a self-description)
- Disruption (unless actually disrupting)
- Scalable (it's assumed)
- Game-changing (show, don't tell)
- AI-powered (what does this even mean anymore?)
- Revolutionary (see: game-changing)

### Industry Terms (Use Correctly)
- LLM vs AI (know the difference)
- Agent vs automation vs workflow
- API, webhook, integration
- Prompt engineering vs system design
- RAG, fine-tuning, context window

## Audience-Specific Adjustments

### Technical Deep Dives (OpenClaw, dev content)
- More code, less context
- Assume familiarity with concepts
- Deep implementation details
- "Here's the exact architecture..."

### Business Case Studies (Stormbreaker, LeadStorm)
- Lead with business outcome
- Technical details as proof, not focus
- ROI and time-to-value metrics
- "What this means for your P&L..."

### Legal Tech (TextEvidence.ai)
- Professional but not stuffy
- Respect the domain expertise
- Show understanding of attorney workflows
- Emphasize accuracy and reliability

### Personal Brand / Builder Stories
- More narrative, reflective
- Behind-the-scenes details
- Lessons learned from failures
- "I used to think... then I learned..."

## CTA Style

### Direct & Specific
- Bad: "Learn more about our services"
- Good: "See how LeadStorm AI qualifies your leads — book a 15-min demo"

### Value-First
- Lead with what they get
- Remove friction (specific time, clear next step)
- Match CTA to content context

### Examples by Venture

**Stormbreaker Digital:** "See how AI agents can run your marketing — let's audit your current workflow"

**TextEvidence.ai:** "Analyze your first case free — upload 10 messages and see the difference"

**LeadStorm AI:** "Stop losing leads to slow response times — see LeadStorm in action"

**OpenClaw:** "Build your first autonomous agent this weekend — grab the starter template"

## Things Matt Would Never Say

- "I'm passionate about..." (shows through action)
- "I have a proven track record..." (results show this)
- "We pride ourselves on..." (cringe corporate speak)
- "In the AI space..." (just say what you mean)
- "Let's circle back" / "Touch base" / "Take this offline"

## Signature Elements

### The Honest Assessment
Include what didn't work or what you'd do differently. Builds trust.

### The Specific Number
Prefer "73%" over "significantly improved"

### The Short Conclusion
End crisp. No summarizing what you just said.

### The Occasional Cigar Reference
Because that's who Matt is.
