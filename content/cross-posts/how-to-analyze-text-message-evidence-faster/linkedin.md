Here are the 5 AI-powered techniques I implement for family law practices:

**1. Intelligent Message Clustering** (30-40% time savings)
Groups messages by thread, topic, and temporal proximity—not forced linear review. Reassembles conversations that span days with gaps.

**2. Relevance Scoring** (50-60% of messages filtered out)
Flags messages likely to be relevant based on case type. Generic AI can't tell "running late, be there in 10" from "you never let me see the kids." Legal-context AI can.

Pro tip: Train on 50-100 messages you've already reviewed. Pattern matching improves dramatically.

**3. Sentiment Trajectory Analysis** (10+ hour patterns surfaced instantly)
Maps emotional tone over time. Identifies escalation, flip-flopping, threats disguised as normal communication.

Real case: 6-week conflict pattern followed by sudden "friendly" period. Cross-referenced with financials = aligned with large withdrawal. Manipulation, not reconciliation.

**4. Entity Extraction** (70% faster cross-referencing)
Auto-catalogs people, locations, dates, financial mentions. Links every related reference.

**5. Automated Summaries** (4-6 hours → 45 minutes)
Case-ready summaries organized by legal relevance, not chronology. Starting with 70% complete draft vs blank page changes everything.
