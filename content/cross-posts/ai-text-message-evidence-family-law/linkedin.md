A family law attorney sent me 847 screenshots from a custody case. She'd spent three weekends organizing them. Still wasn't sure she'd caught every important pattern.

I built TextEvidence.ai to solve this. Here's how AI actually analyzes text message evidence for family law—and why it finds patterns humans miss.

**The problem:** A typical contested divorce involves 5,000+ messages. Manual review is impractical, yet critical patterns hide in that volume.

Traditional approaches fail because:
❌ Screenshots lack conversation context
❌ Important exchanges get buried in irrelevant chatter
❌ Humans struggle to correlate across platforms (iMessage, WhatsApp, SMS)
❌ No pattern detection for escalation trends or harassment cycles

**How AI analysis works:**

**1. Chronological Reconstruction**
Parses different export formats, extracts timestamps, handles timezone conversions, reconstructs conversation threads. Every message gets a line number for easy court citation.

**2. Entity Extraction & Relationship Mapping**
Identifies participants across formats—even when names aren't explicit. Correlates phone numbers, emails, handles. Reveals triangulation patterns (messages about third parties) that direct review misses.

**3. Sentiment Trajectory Analysis**
Not just positive/negative. Tracks emotional intensity, response latency, topic persistence, language formality shifts over time.

Real application: Instead of saying "the respondent became increasingly hostile," present data showing 340% increase in aggressive language over 6 weeks, correlated with triggering events.

**4. Behavioral Pattern Recognition**
• Harassment cycling (repeated contact after requests to stop)
• Parental gatekeeping patterns
• Compliance tracking for court-ordered boundaries
• Substance indication (unusual timestamps + language patterns)

**Court-ready output includes:**
✓ Line-numbered exhibits for citations
✓ Metadata preservation with hash verification
✓ Explainable reports (not black box)
✓ PDF exhibits, chronological narratives, pattern summaries

**What AI CAN'T do:**
❌ Contextual interpretation (attorneys know which "order" is referenced)
❌ Authentication verification
❌ Strategic decisions about presentation
❌ Ethical boundary evaluation

AI augments attorney judgment. It doesn't replace it.

The goal: Eliminate tedious evidence organization so attorneys can focus on strategy, advocacy, and client counseling.
